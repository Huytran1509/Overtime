The Women's Tennis Association has implemented a new eligibility policy requiring all players to undergo a one-time SRY gene test via blood sample or cheek swab as a condition of competing in any WTA tournament, including Grand Slams. The policy took effect on July 21, 2026, and a positive test result renders a player ineligible unless she can demonstrate a qualifying Difference of Sexual Development. The WTA said the policy is intended to maintain fair competition and was introduced without a public press release, drawing significant attention from players and medical professionals. The move follows similar rules adopted by World Athletics and World Boxing in 2025.
