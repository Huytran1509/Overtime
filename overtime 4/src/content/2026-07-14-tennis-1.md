Jannik Sinner successfully defended his Wimbledon men's singles title on July 12, coming from a set down to defeat Alexander Zverev 6-7, 7-6, 6-3, 6-4 in three hours and 46 minutes on Centre Court. The victory gave the Italian world No. 1 his fifth career Grand Slam title and made him the 10th man in the Open Era to win back-to-back Wimbledon crowns. Zverev became the first German man to contest the Wimbledon final since Boris Becker in 1995.
