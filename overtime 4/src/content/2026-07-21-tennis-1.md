Jannik Sinner successfully defended his Wimbledon men's singles title on July 12, coming from a set down to beat Alexander Zverev 6-7, 7-6, 6-3, 6-4, claiming his fifth Grand Slam trophy. The Italian became the 10th man in Open Era history to win back-to-back Wimbledon titles and also recorded his 100th career Grand Slam match win. With Carlos Alcaraz still sidelined by injury, Sinner now turns his attention to the upcoming hard-court swing, with his coaching team indicating he is likely to compete at the Canadian Open in Montreal in August. He needs four more Masters 1000 titles to complete a feat no player has ever achieved.
