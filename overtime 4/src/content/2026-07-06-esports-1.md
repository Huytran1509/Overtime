The Esports World Cup 2026 officially kicked off in Paris, France on July 6, marking the first time the event has been held outside Saudi Arabia. The seven-week tournament runs until August 23 and features 25 events across 24 games, including VALORANT, Dota 2, Counter-Strike 2, and Apex Legends, with a record total prize pool of 75 million US dollars. Saudi club Team Falcons enters with the broadest competitive footprint, having qualified for 20 of the 25 events, while home side Team Vitality qualified for 16.
