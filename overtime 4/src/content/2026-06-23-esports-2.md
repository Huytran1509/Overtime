The Esports Nations Cup 2026, set to take place in Riyadh from November 2-29, is facing serious questions about its credibility after the Korea Esports Association (KeSPA) and the Esports Foundation ended their partnership over an alleged dispute involving organizers pushing for specific high-profile players to be included in South Korea's national roster. China's participation is also reportedly unconfirmed, leaving the tournament without two of the world's most dominant esports regions. The Korean Sport and Olympic Committee warned that any team assembled outside official channels cannot use the South Korean flag or the Team Korea name. A subsequent update from a Leaguepedia nations list noted that KeSPA and ENC have since officially re-confirmed their partnership, though the situation remains closely watched.
