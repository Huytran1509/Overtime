Arthur Fery, a 23-year-old wildcard, defeated veteran Grigor Dimitrov in a dramatic five-set match on Centre Court to reach the Wimbledon quarterfinals for the first time, becoming the first wildcard to do so in the men's draw since Nick Kyrgios in 2014. Fery came back from two sets down against Dimitrov, winning the deciding fifth set in a tiebreak buoyed by a raucous home crowd. His run is considered all the more remarkable given that he had been coming back from a set down in all of his previous matches as well.
