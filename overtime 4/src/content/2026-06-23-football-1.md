Lionel Messi became the highest goal scorer in World Cup history on June 22 by netting his 17th and 18th career tournament goals in Argentina's 2-0 win over Austria in Dallas. The record-breaking 17th goal came in the 38th minute, just two days before his 39th birthday, surpassing Germany's Miroslav Klose who held the previous record of 16 goals. Messi also surpassed Brazil's Marta to lead the all-time scoring charts across both men's and women's World Cups. Argentina secured qualification to the knockout round, with Messi having scored all five of his country's goals in the tournament so far.
