The 2026 Esports World Cup officially gets underway in Paris, France, with its opening ceremony set for July 8 at La Seine Musicale, following the start of competition on July 6. The event was relocated from Riyadh, Saudi Arabia to Paris earlier this year and will feature 25 tournaments across 24 esports titles, with a record $75 million total prize pool. Team Falcons leads all organizations in event participation, having qualified for 20 of the 25 competitions.
