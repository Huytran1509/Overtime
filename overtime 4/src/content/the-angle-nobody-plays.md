There is a shot that does not show up in the highlight reel because it does not end the point. It *sets up* the point — and it is the most underrated weapon in the modern game.

## Bending the court

A tennis court is a rectangle, but players experience it as a set of angles, and angles can be widened. The inside-out forehand — stepping around the backhand to drive cross-court — does exactly that. It does not aim for a line. It aims for *the next ball*.

| Shot | Visible reward | Real reward |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Down the line | Spectacular | Risky, low margin |
| Inside-out | Quiet | Opens the whole court |

## Why patience reads as power

The player who hits three inside-out forehands in a row is not being conservative. They are tilting the rally a few degrees at a time until the opponent is standing in the wrong half of the court — and then the easy ball appears, as if by accident.

> The angle nobody plays is the one that doesn't win the point. It wins the *position*. The point comes for free after that.

Watch the best baseliners and you'll stop seeing winners. You'll start seeing the two shots before the winner, where the real work was done.
