Former Wimbledon champion Marketa Vondrousova has been suspended for four years by the International Tennis Integrity Agency after refusing to open her door to a doping control officer in December 2025. An independent tribunal found that her explanations — citing stress, poor mental health and concerns about the tester's identity — provided no compelling justification for the refusal. The 26-year-old Czech, who has never tested positive for a banned substance, vehemently denied any wrongdoing and faces the prospect of not returning to the tour until 2030.
